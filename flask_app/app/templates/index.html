Habitat Hero!

Instructions for Application

1. Upload an image of a wildlife animal using the "Choose File" button. This can either be a single animal or a group of animals.
2. Click the "Upload" button to submit the image.
3. You will be redirected to another page where the application will display the name(s) of the detected animal(s) in the image as well as the original image with bounding boxes around the animals in the image.
4. Click on the name of a detected animal to garner more general information about the animal as well as its endangered status.
5. Ask the chatbot questions to learn more about the animal, its habitat, conservation status, and ways we as humans can help.

Methodologies Used

1. The application uses a fine-tuned YOLOv5 object detection model to detect wildlife animals in images that the user uploads.
2. Relevant chunks of information regarding the detected animals are extracted from the MongoDB Atlas database for RAG purposes and prompt-engineering.
3. The relevant chunks are then passed as context to OpenAI's GPT-3.5 Turbo to generate information to the user regarding the animal of focus.

Purpose

On a global level, there are unfortunately many commonly known species such as elephants, rhinos, gorillas, etc. that are now endangered and face extinction due to a lack of public awareness and conservation engagement. Although there are tools in place to help mitigate the chance of extinction of these endangered species, there is a severe lack of educational tools that give the public a broader understanding of the problem as well as potential solutions. This project helps bridge this gap by allowing the public to learn more about a wide variety of wild-life animals and potential ways we as humans can help animals around the world thrive. The wildlife animals that the model primarily focuses on are: antelope, bat, bear, bee, butterfly, Domestic short-haired cats, chimpanzee, coyote, dolphin, eagle, elephant, gorilla, hippopotamus, rhinoceros, hummingbird, kangaroo, koala, leopard, lion, lizard, orangutan, panda, penguin, seal, shark, tiger, turtle, whale, and zebra.

The concept of using computer vision to identify or classify animals is not inherently novel. There have been research papers in the past that have attempted to utilize computer vision for animal detection. For example, “The iNaturalist Species Classification and Detection Dataset” by Grant Van Horn et al. is a groundbreaking research paper that focuses on classification of different species via a large dataset of animals. Feel free to check out the paper to learn more about interesting CV methods being used in the field of wildlife conservation.
